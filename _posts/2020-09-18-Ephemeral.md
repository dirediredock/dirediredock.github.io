title: "#001 - Coal Harbour's lost kelp island"
---

Admiralty map from 1890 has glyphs that represent kelp forests. The red circle shows an ephemeral low tide island of Coal Harbour with this glyph.


![](/images/Ephemeral1.png)

Modern bathymetry shows the same island is still there. However, kelp does not occur there today, nor anywhere in the Inner Harbour region. This is likely due to decades of urban development that stressed kelp forests towards local extinction.


![](/images/Ephemeral3.png)
