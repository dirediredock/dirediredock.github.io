This is a research notebook for a Mitacs PhD fellowship project at the The University of British Columbia (UBC), in partnership with Tsleil-Waututh Nation (TWN) and Kerr Wood Leidal Associates Ltd (KWL). This project explores 200 years of industrial change through ecological models, part of TWN Cumulative Effects Monitoring Initiative (CEMI). Today Burrard Inlet hosts Canada’s largest and busiest port, part of the city of Metro Vancouver, while simultaneously remaining the main waterway and ancestral territory of TWN, inhabitants of the inlet since time immemorial.
